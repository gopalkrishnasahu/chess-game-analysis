# Chess Analyser — Growth & Distribution Action Items

These are things *you* do to get the app in front of players.
Separate from app feature work (see ACTION_PLAN.md).

---

## Before You Share Anything

- [ ] Get the app live on Render.com (5 min setup — see ACTION_PLAN.md)
- [ ] Run your own analysis and screenshot the report — you need a real example to show people
- [ ] Pick a name and get a domain (~$12/yr on Namecheap) — even just pointing it to your Render URL makes it look real
- [ ] Write one sentence that explains what it does: *"Paste your Lichess or Chess.com username — get a free report showing your patterns across your last 50 games, not just one game."*

---

## Reddit (Highest ROI, Do This First)

Chess communities actively share tools. One good post = hundreds of users overnight.

- [ ] **r/chess** — post your analysis screenshot + the one-liner. Title like:
  *"I built a free tool that analyses your last 50 games and tells you exactly where you're losing — feedback welcome"*
- [ ] **r/chessbeginners** — slightly different angle:
  *"Made a free tool to help improvers find their bad habits across many games, not just one"*
- [ ] **r/lichess** — mention Lichess support specifically, community is very receptive to tools built on their API
- [ ] **r/chesscom** — mention Chess.com support

> **Tips:**
> - Post on a weekday morning (US time) when traffic is highest
> - Lead with your own report screenshot — real output builds trust instantly
> - Be upfront that you built it — chess Reddit likes genuine builders
> - Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours

---

## YouTube

Long shelf life. People search "how to improve at chess" and "analyse chess games" constantly.

- [ ] Record a 3–5 minute screen recording: open the app, type your username, watch it run, walk through the report
- [ ] Title ideas:
  - *"Free tool that finds your chess weaknesses across 50 games (not just one)"*
  - *"Analyse your chess patterns for free — Lichess + Chess.com"*
- [ ] Pin a comment with the link
- [ ] No need for a camera — screen + voiceover is fine

---

## Chess Forums & Communities

- [ ] **Lichess forum** → "Game analysis" section — post about the tool
- [ ] **Chess.com forums** → "Chess Tools, Chess Websites" section
- [ ] **Chess Stack Exchange** — answer questions about "how to analyse my games" with a mention of the tool

---

## Twitter / X

- [ ] Post your report screenshot with:
  *"Built a free chess analysis tool — shows your patterns across 50 games. Works with Lichess + Chess.com. No sign-up."*
- [ ] Tag @lichess — they occasionally retweet community tools
- [ ] Use hashtags: #chess #chessimprovement #chesscommunity

---

## When You Have 10–20 Users

- [ ] Ask them to share their report (add a "Share this report" button in the app)
- [ ] Ask for feedback — what's confusing, what's missing
- [ ] One testimonial quote on the landing page doubles conversion

---

## Slow Burn (Do Later, Not Now)

These take months but compound over time:

- [ ] Write a blog post: *"The 5 most common patterns in 1400-rated chess games"* (analyse data from real users with permission)
- [ ] Submit to tool directories: Awesome Chess GitHub lists, Product Hunt, Hacker News "Show HN"
- [ ] Reach out to chess YouTube creators with <50k subscribers — more likely to respond, often looking for tools to feature

---

## What NOT to Spend Time On

- ❌ Building social media followers from scratch (too slow)
- ❌ Google Ads (expensive, chess tool CPCs are high)
- ❌ SEO articles (months before any effect — do Reddit first)
- ❌ A fancy landing page before you have real user feedback

---

## The One Thing

If you only do one thing: **post on r/chess with a real screenshot of your own report.**
That single action will tell you within 48 hours whether people want this.
Everything else follows from that signal.
